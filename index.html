I'm Tony and I'm a microbiologist data scientist.

Well, I was a microbiologist in a previous life. Didn't learn anything particularly useful other than that antibiotic soap is bad and immune cells are good.

After graduation, I entered a Masters program in computational biology studying Epigenetics. Very esoteric, right? Anyways, it turns out analyzing gigabytes of data, finding needles of value in haystacks of data, and telling interesting stories with data are highly transferrable skills. Luckily, I've had plenty of oppurtuinties to transfer them. Now my interests lie in solving different data science problems across a variety of domains. I'm particularily good at exploratory data analysis and unsupervised learning problems, mainly because most datasets in biology are unlabeled. And unexplored.

Along the way, I somehow learned how to do basic web development. I can create basic web applications or make simple websites to visualize data. Turns out this is a very useful skill to have when communicating with non-experts. This skill also enabled me to win 4 hackathons (netting $9,000 CAD in prize money)
