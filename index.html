I'm Tony and I'm a microbiologist data scientist.

Well, I was a microbiologist in a previous life. Didn't learn anything particularily useful other than that antibiotic soap is bad and immune cells are good.

So I did something about it and entered a Masters program in computational biology studying Epigenetics. Very esoteric, right? Anyway, turns out analyzing gigabytes of epigenetic data is a highly transferrable skill. And so I've transferred them. Now my interests lie in solving data science problems in different domains. I'm particularly good at exploratory data analysis and unsupervised learning problems, mainly because most datasets in biology are unlabeled. And unexplored.

Along the way, I learned how to do basic web development. I can create simple websites for simple applications or to visualize data. Turns out this is a very useful skill to have when communicating with non-experts.

But most importantly, I'm a scientist. I am objective, methodological, and calculating in experiments, including "data experiments". In my opinion, "science" part of data science is woefully underemphasized.
